Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about menstrual health, reproductive wellness, and more.

Menstrual Cycle

What is a normal period?

A normal period typically lasts 3-7 days with a menstrual cycle of 21-35 days. The average cycle is 28 days, but this varies from person to person. Normal menstrual flow ranges from light to heavy and may include blood clots.

If your period consistently lasts longer than 7 days or is extremely heavy (soaking through protection every hour), you should consult a healthcare provider.

How do I track my period?

You can track your period using our tracker page. Here's how:

  1. Navigate to the Period Tracker page
  2. Record the start date of your period
  3. Log symptoms and flow intensity
  4. Add the end date once your period is over

Tracking for at least 3-4 cycles will help provide more accurate predictions for your next period.

What are the signs of ovulation?

Common signs of ovulation include:

  • Changes in cervical mucus (becomes clearer, slippery, and stretchy like egg white)
  • Mild pelvic or lower abdominal pain (mittelschmerz)
  • Slight increase in basal body temperature
  • Increased sex drive
  • Light spotting
  • Breast tenderness
  • Heightened sense of smell

Ovulation typically occurs around the middle of your cycle, but this can vary.

Can stress affect my menstrual cycle?

Yes, stress can significantly impact your menstrual cycle. High levels of stress can cause:

  • Delayed periods
  • Missed periods (amenorrhea)
  • Irregular cycles
  • Changes in period duration
  • More painful periods (dysmenorrhea)

This happens because stress activates your body's fight-or-flight response and can affect the hypothalamus, which regulates hormones controlling your menstrual cycle.

Managing stress through exercise, meditation, adequate sleep, and other self-care practices can help regulate your cycle.

Sexual Health

Is it safe to have sex during my period?

Yes, it is generally safe to have sex during menstruation. Some people even find that having sex during this time can help relieve cramps due to the release of endorphins.

However, it's important to note that:

  • You can still get pregnant during your period, especially if you have a shorter cycle
  • You can still contract or transmit sexually transmitted infections (STIs) during your period
  • Using condoms or other barriers is still recommended if pregnancy or STIs are concerns

For comfort and cleanliness, you might consider laying a dark towel underneath you or having sex in the shower.

What birth control options are available?

There are many birth control options with varying effectiveness rates:

  • Hormonal methods: pills, patches, rings, injections, implants (91-99% effective)
  • Barrier methods: condoms, diaphragms, cervical caps (71-98% effective)
  • Intrauterine devices (IUDs): hormonal and copper (>99% effective)
  • Emergency contraception: morning-after pills, copper IUD insertion
  • Permanent methods: tubal ligation, vasectomy (>99% effective)
  • Natural methods: fertility awareness-based methods, withdrawal (76-96% effective)

Consulting with a healthcare provider can help determine which option is best for you based on your health history, lifestyle, and preferences.

How do I know if I have a yeast infection?

Common symptoms of a vaginal yeast infection include:

  • Itching and irritation in the vagina and vulva
  • Burning sensation, especially during urination or intercourse
  • Redness and swelling of the vulva
  • Vaginal pain or soreness
  • Thick, white, odorless discharge with a cottage cheese-like appearance

If you're experiencing these symptoms for the first time, it's best to see a healthcare provider for a proper diagnosis. Other infections can have similar symptoms but require different treatments.

Using the Tracker

How accurate are period predictions?

Period predictions become more accurate the more data you input. After tracking for 3-4 cycles, the app can provide reasonably accurate predictions for most regular cycles.

However, keep in mind that many factors can affect your cycle, including:

  • Stress
  • Travel
  • Illness
  • Changes in weight or exercise habits
  • Medical conditions like PCOS

Even with regular tracking, predictions may sometimes be off by a few days.

How can I back up my tracking data?

Currently, your data is stored locally in your browser's storage. To avoid losing your data:

  • Don't clear your browser data/cache for this website
  • Use the same device and browser to access the tracker

In the future, we plan to add account-based storage that will allow you to access your data across different devices.
